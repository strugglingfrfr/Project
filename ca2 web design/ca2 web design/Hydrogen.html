Ocean Power: Tidal and Wave Energy

These renewable sources harness ocean motion to produce low-carbon electricity, supporting global clean energy goals and sustainable development efforts.

Learn more

What is Tidal and Wave energy?

Tidal and wave energy are renewable sources that use ocean motion to generate electricity. Tidal energy comes from gravitational forces creating tidal currents, while wave energy harnesses the movement of surface water. Both are sustainable and eco-friendly alternatives to fossil fuels​


 

Why is tidal energy considered a reliable renewable source?

Tidal energy is reliable because tidal patterns are driven by the predictable gravitational forces of the moon and sun, ensuring consistent energy production. Unlike wind or solar energy, which can be intermittent, tidal energy provides a stable and regular power source
