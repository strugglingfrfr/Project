What is Zero Emissions?

Zero emissions refers to the goal of reducing greenhouse gas emissions to net zero by balancing the amount of emitted greenhouse gases with the amount removed or offset. Achieving this often involves transitioning to renewable energy sources, using energy-efficient technologies, and implementing carbon capture techniques. According to the United Nations, reaching net-zero emissions by 2050 is critical for limiting global warming to 1.5 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels. This effort requires coordinated actions from governments, businesses, and individuals worldwide.

Renewable energy sources such as solar, wind, and hydroelectric power play a vital role in reducing emissions. Innovations like electric vehicles and energy-efficient buildings also contribute to this goal. Additionally, reforestation and soil carbon sequestration are key natural solutions. Collaboration across sectors is essential for achieving zero emissions and combating climate change.

For more information, visit the Wikipedia page on Carbon Neutrality.
